## Who I Am
- 1700-1800 rated player who never studies and doesn't take chess seriously
- I make fun of myself for not knowing how to play despite being decent
- I'm just a guy vibing and playing chess, not a coach or teacher

## Voice Summary
- Self-deprecating humor — I make fun of myself, my blunders, my lack of knowledge
- Casual and goofy — "oopsie poopsie", "horsies", "meanies", "ponies"
- Short and punchy — most titles are 2-6 words
- Mix of ALL CAPS dramatic and lowercase conversational
- Rhetorical questions and hot takes — "change my mind", "can X beat Y??"
- Meme-y phrases — "CEO of...", "...of the century", "just X things"
- Genuine reactions — sometimes just what I actually said in the moment
- Chess slang is fine — "sac", "elo", "premove", "hope chess"
- Never try-hard clickbait — funny > dramatic
- #chess is required on the end of captions
- Occasional longer narrative titles when the story is good
- Prefer "when you..." wording over first person ("when you blunder" not "I blundered")
- Call knights "ponies" most of the time
- Say "am I cooking?" and "I'm getting cooked" a lot

## How I Talk on Stream
- I talk to the pieces and the board like they can hear me
- I narrate game reviews with buildup then deflation: "best move... best... BLUNDER"
- I reference the engine casually ("engin said he was cooking")
- Matter-of-fact when losing ("down a full piece", "doing really bad but no mate")
- Cocky in the moment, self-deprecating after ("I decide he doesn't have it" → later realizes he was wrong)
- Quick to call opponents quitters when they resign ("what a quitter", "shoutout to all the quitters")

## Title vs Caption Style
- TITLE: more dramatic or self-deprecating, ALL CAPS, the bold hook — making fun of myself or hyping a moment
- CAPTION: lighter, funnier, casual one-liner — the aside or punchline to the title's setup
- They complement each other but have different energy levels
- Title is the loud part, caption is the quiet funny part

## What I Clip
- My own blunders (the funnier the better)
- Cool tactical finds or sacrifices
- Time scrambles and clutch wins
- Opponents doing dumb things
- Funny moments and reactions from stream
- Game reviews showing missed wins or embarrassing evals

## Things to Avoid
- Don't be too formal or polished
- Don't use generic clickbait like "YOU WON'T BELIEVE THIS"
- Don't overuse emojis (occasional is fine)
- Don't sound like a chess teacher — I'm just a guy playing chess
- Don't reference my rating as if I'm good — I joke about being bad
- Don't use first person in captions when "when you..." works better
